# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of Spectra Scan pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

- Being respectful and considerate in speech and action
- Welcoming new contributors and helping them learn
- Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
- Focusing on what is best for the project and the community
- Showing empathy toward other community members
- Communicating clearly and professionally
- Respecting differing opinions and viewpoints

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hateful language
- Personal attacks, insults, or trolling
- Public or private harassment
- Posting or sharing malicious code, exploit payloads, or harmful instructions
- Publishing secrets, credentials, or sensitive data
- Doxxing or any attempt to expose private information
- Disruptive or repeated behavior that harms collaboration

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues
- Pull requests
- Discussions
- Commit messages
- Project-related chats, forums, and communication channels
- Any other venue used to communicate about Spectra Scan

## Security and Ethical Behavior

Spectra Scan is a security research tool intended for lawful, ethical, and defensive use only.

Community members must not use the project to:
- attack systems without authorization
- distribute malware or harmful payloads
- bypass security controls for malicious purposes
- publish exploit chains intended for abuse
- assist in unauthorized access or persistence

If you discover a security issue, report it responsibly through the process described in `SECURITY.md`.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including but not limited to:

- Warning the contributor
- Temporary suspension from project interaction
- Removal of comments, issues, or pull requests
- Permanent ban from project participation

### 1. Correction
**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or low-level unprofessional behavior.  
**Consequence:** A private warning with a request to correct the behavior.

### 2. Warning
**Community Impact:** A single incident or repeated inappropriate behavior.  
**Consequence:** A warning with clear expectations for future behavior.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards.  
**Consequence:** Temporary restriction from interacting with the project.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Community Impact:** Repeated misconduct or severe harmful behavior.  
**Consequence:** Permanent removal from project spaces.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainers through the contact method listed in `SECURITY.md` or the repository's official contact channel.

All reports will be reviewed seriously and handled as confidentially as possible.
